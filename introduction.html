Green hydrogen is a type of hydrogen fuel that is produced using renewable energy sources. Unlike conventional hydrogen production methods, which often rely on natural gas or other fossil fuels and result in significant carbon emissions, green hydrogen is produced through the process of electrolysis. This process splits water (H2O) into hydrogen (H2) and oxygen (O2) using electricity generated by renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, or hydropower.
