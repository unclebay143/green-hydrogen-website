Challenges :

Cost: The production of green hydrogen is currently more expensive than conventional methods due to the high cost of electrolysis technology and renewable energy infrastructure.

Infrastructure: Limited distribution and storage infrastructure can make widespread adoption challenging

Efficiency: The overall energy conversion process (from electricity to hydrogen and back to electricity) has lower efficiency compared to direct use of electricity.

Renewable Energy Dependency: Green hydrogen requires large amounts of renewable energy, which may strain already limited renewable energy resources needed for direct electrification.

Water Consumption: Electrolysis requires significant quantities of purified water, which could be a concern in water-scarce regions
